I'm Andrew,
but you can call me Drew.

I am a freelance audio engineer with a diverse technical and artistic skillset, including audio and music production, video production, physical computing, web design, graphic design, and photography.

I have a passion for storytelling by combining various media to create a larger, interconnected narrative. I am always trying to get back to the magic of that voice memo demo while also creating a work that sounds polished and professional. My goal is to do more with less. What lies at the core of every project I undertake is the story that it is telling, through its content.

Below are some of the works I am most proud of. It is my hope that together, they become larger than the sum of their parts.

Busking at the Funeral

I write, record and produce music through the moniker, Adrew Jacob. I am always working on new music, but my most recent project is a 9 song EP titled Busking at the Funeral.

I also released a stripped version of this EP, consisting of four bare bones versions of songs from Busking at the Funeral, and one original single, titled Sirens.

Sirens

Sirens, takes inspiration from the myth in The Odyssey. To achieve the reverb effect on the guitar for this song and the Busking at the Funeral (Stripped) Project, I used an Organelle by Critter & Guitari as a preamp.

Litany for Winter & Spring

This is my most recent Single, titled Litany for Winter and Spring. It explores the strange coexistence of hope and despair in the inbetween place between Winter and Spring.

Chase the Light

Chase the Light is an older project. It is an autobiographical story of my changing perspective on the religion I grew up in.

Below are three songs from an upcoming double album of the same name: Chase the Light, Paper Towns, and Up with The Birds.

Covers

To better understand one of my favorite songs and artists, I covered and released Heavenly Father by Bon Iver. You can listen to it below.

Below that is a cover of Banks by NEEDTOBREATHE, performed by a collaborator and fellow musician named Nick Kozlarek. He was not able to make it to a mutual friend's wedding, and so he recorded his vocals and guitar while he was overseas and sent them over to me. I then produced the rest of the track, and it was played for the first dance at the wedding. It was a way for him to be a part of the ceremony when he couldn't be there in person.

The Psalm/Song Podcast

I know. It's a terrible name. But regardless, this 16 episode podcast was a passion project of mine and is very dear to me. Each episode is a short discussion of how a specific artist blurs the lines between sacred and secular through their music, and finishes with a cover of one of their songs, performed by me.

The podcast features music from Mondo Cozmo, Gary Clark Jr., The Mowgli's, Judah and the Lion, The Collection, Bear's Den, The Whistles & The Bells, Julien Baker, Jon Bellion, Colony House, Chance the Rapper, Kendrick Lamar, and U2. You can watch a teaser video for the first eight episodes below.

Episodes 1-6

Episodes 1-6 feature discussion of Mondo Cozmo, Gary Clark Jr., The Mowgli's, Judah and the Lion, The Collection, and Bear's Den.

Episodes 7-12

Episodes 7-12 feature discussions of The Whistles & The Bells, Julien Baker, Jon Bellion, Colony House, and Chance the Rapper.

Episodes 13-16

Episodes 13-16 feature discussions of Kendrick Lamar and U2. Then the podcast closes out with a comparison between Kendrick Lamar and Chance the Rapper, as well as a comparison between Kendrick Lamar and U2.
